Health Measure Details

  • Pregnancies: The number of times a woman has been pregnant. This factor is considered because higher pregnancies can indicate increased risk of diabetes. (Unit: Count)
  • Glucose: Plasma glucose concentration at 2 hours during an oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT). Higher glucose levels are associated with a higher likelihood of diabetes. (Unit: mg/dL)
  • Blood Pressure: The diastolic blood pressure value (measured in mm Hg). High blood pressure can often correlate with diabetes. (Unit: mm Hg)
  • Skin Thickness: Skinfold thickness in millimeters. This measure is an indicator of body fat and is correlated with insulin resistance. (Unit: mm)
  • Insulin: Insulin levels in the blood after a 2-hour test. Low or high insulin levels could indicate the presence of diabetes. (Unit: µU/mL)
  • BMI: Body mass index (weight in kg / height in m²). A BMI higher than 23 is generally considered a risk factor for diabetes. (Unit: kg/m²)
  • Diabetes Pedigree Function: A function that scores the likelihood of diabetes based on family history and other genetic factors. (Unit: None, score between 0-1)
  • Age: Age in years. The risk of diabetes increases with age, especially after 45 years. (Unit: Years)
